A Little About Me…

I’m a fourth-year PhD student in Industrial Engineering & Operations Research, advised by Prof. Henry Lam. My work focuses on uncertainty quantification and decision-making under uncertainty—developing methods that remain reliable when data or environments shift.

Before Columbia, I completed my bachelor’s and master’s at the Indian Statistical Institute, with a master’s focus on computational statistics. I also bring industry perspective from Capital One and other roles across risk management, data modeling, and analytics—experience that helps me frame research questions with practical constraints in mind.

Outside work, I enjoy chess, table tennis, and puzzle solving. I also love to travel—long walks through new neighborhoods often nudge how I think about structure, systems, and the way people move through the world.

If you’re exploring robustness, data-driven optimization, or adjacent topics, I’m always open to a conversation.

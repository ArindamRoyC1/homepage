A Little About Me…

I’m a fourth-year PhD student at Columbia University in the Department of Industrial Engineering & Operations Research (IEOR), advised by Prof. Henry Lam. My research focuses on uncertainty quantification and decision-making under uncertainty—developing methods that remain reliable when data or environments shift.

My academic background includes bachelor’s and master’s degrees in Statistics from the Indian Statistical Institute, Kolkata. I bring industry experience from my time at Capital One, where I worked on modeling loan default rates and developed tools for handling missing values in decision-tree models. I also spent a summer working with NASA Langley Research Centre, where I focussed on multivariate flight data modeling.

Outside work, I enjoy chess, table tennis, and puzzle solving. I also love to travel—long walks through new neighborhoods often nudge how I think about structure, systems, and the way people move through the world.

If you’re exploring robustness, data-driven optimization, or adjacent topics, I’m always open to a conversation.

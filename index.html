A Little About Me…

I’m a fourth-year PhD student at Columbia University in the Department of Industrial Engineering & Operations Research (IEOR), advised by Prof. Henry Lam. My research focuses on uncertainty quantification and decision-making under uncertainty—developing methods that remain reliable when data or environments shift.

My academic background includes bachelor’s and master’s degrees in Statistics from the Indian Statistical Institute, Kolkata. I bring industry experience from my time at Capital One, where I worked on modeling loan default rates and developed tools for handling missing values in decision-tree models. I also spent a summer working with the NASA Langley Research Center, where I focused on multivariate flight data modeling.

Outside work, I enjoy chess, table tennis, and puzzle solving. I also love to travel and often spend time on long walks through new neighborhoods, enjoying the chance to explore and observe everyday life in different places.

If you’re exploring robustness, data-driven optimization, or adjacent topics, I’m always open to a conversation.
